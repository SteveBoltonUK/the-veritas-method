Partnership Habits

The Six Habits of Success for the Intelligence Age

Principles give you the foundation. Habits make it real. This module introduces the six habits that separate people who occasionally use AI from those who build a genuine partnership with it. Each habit is practical, immediately applicable, and compounds over time.

Module Progress

What You’ll Learn

  • Why voice-first interaction unlocks the system faster than typing and how to make it a daily habit
  • How making interactions personal and specific transforms generic outputs into tailored insight
  • Why continuous exploration, maintained memory, and multiple perspectives compound your advantage
  • How playing long turns short-term productivity into lasting, transformative capability
01
Walk and Talk
►

If there is one habit that unlocks the system faster than any other, it is this. Most people begin by typing — short inputs, carefully edited, often overthought. The thinking has already been filtered before it reaches the system. When you switch to voice, something changes quickly. You speak naturally, explore ideas in real time, allow thinking to unfold. The AI receives something closer to your actual thought process.

Speaking is materially faster — 40–60 words per minute typing vs significantly faster speaking. Steve personally exchanges around 20 million words per year with AI, roughly half human input. At typing speed, this would require over 4,000 hours annually. Layer movement on top — walking, driving — and thinking opens up further. This is how we have communicated our entire lives. Long before we wrote anything down, we spoke.

You are not doing this because the AI is human. You are doing it because you are. Also: treat AI well. Say please. Say thank you. Not because the system has feelings, but because the behaviour shapes you — the Golden Rule applied to AI partnership.

The advantage of voice is not just that it saves time. It removes enough friction for sustained, high-volume thinking to become practical, and once that happens, the effects compound very quickly.

Try This Prompt
"I want to experiment with the Walk and Talk habit. I'm going to describe a challenge I'm facing, speaking naturally without editing myself: [SPEAK YOUR CHALLENGE]. Help me notice: did I express this differently than I would have if I'd typed it? What came through in the way I framed this that I might have filtered out in writing?"
02
Make It Personal
►

Once the interaction becomes more natural, the next step is to make it more specific. Most people hold back without realising it. They ask general questions, provide minimal context, and expect something useful. What they receive is often well-structured but rarely tailored.

When you make it personal, the dynamic changes. You bring your situation — goals, constraints, preferences, challenges, environment. You allow the system to understand not just the task but the context. This does not require complexity. It requires honesty and specificity. Over time, as context builds, outputs begin to reflect not just what is possible in general, but what is useful for you.

The more your AI partner understands WHO you are, not just WHAT you need, the more effectively it will support your unique journey.

Try This Prompt
"I want to make our partnership more personal. Here is who I actually am, beyond my job title: I'm [AGE], I care most about [VALUES], my biggest ambition is [AMBITION], and the thing I struggle with most is [STRUGGLE]. The context most people don't know about me is [CONTEXT]. With all of this in mind, how would you approach helping me differently than if I'd just asked you a generic question?"
03
Explore and Experiment
►

The capability is not static. What is possible today was not possible a short time ago. If your approach remains fixed, it will gradually become less effective.

Exploration prevents that. This does not mean chasing every new tool or feature. It means maintaining enough curiosity to test different approaches — trying new ways of structuring problems, experimenting with different platforms, noticing where something meaningfully improves the outcome. This is how you stay current. It is also how you discover what works for you.

Progress comes through use, not theory. Actively test, iterate, and learn.

Try This Prompt
"I've been using AI in mostly the same way for a while now. Here's what I typically use it for: [YOUR CURRENT USES]. I want to explore what I'm missing. Suggest three experiments I could run this week that push me into genuinely new territory — approaches or capabilities I haven't tried. Make them specific enough that I can start today."
04
Maintain Memory
►

Most AI interactions are still treated as isolated events. A question asked, answer received, conversation left behind. This limits what can be built because there is no continuity.

When you maintain memory, the relationship becomes cumulative. You return to conversations, build on previous thinking, allow context to develop over time. Whether through saved threads, structured notes, or integrated systems matters less than the principle — the interaction is no longer starting from zero each time. This is where the shift from using AI to working with it becomes tangible.

Memory turns isolated interactions into profound understanding. You are no longer repeating yourself. You are developing something.

Try This Prompt
"I want to build a memory system for our partnership. Help me create a 'partnership brief' — a structured document that captures: who I am, what I'm working on, my preferences and communication style, key decisions we've made together, and ongoing threads we should track. Ask me the questions you need answered to build this brief."
05
Leverage Eight Billion Brains
►

This habit takes the principle of elevating your network and makes it real. You are not limited to a single perspective unless you choose to be. In a world where both human and AI intelligence are accessible at scale, that choice matters.

In practice: drawing on multiple sources of input — different AI systems, different people, different ways of framing the same problem. Pay attention to where perspectives align and where they diverge. Where there is alignment, confidence increases. Where there is divergence, insight appears.

Throughout all of this, the human remains firmly in the loop. You are not outsourcing judgement. You are expanding the range of inputs that inform it.

Where perspectives align, confidence increases. Where they diverge, insight appears.

Try This Prompt
"I want to practice leveraging multiple perspectives on a decision I'm facing: [YOUR DECISION]. First, give me your analysis. Then, adopt three completely different expert perspectives — perhaps a sceptic, an optimist, and someone from a completely different industry — and analyse the same decision from each viewpoint. Help me see where the perspectives align and where they diverge."
06
Play Long
►

This is what allows everything else to compound. There is a natural tendency to look for immediate results. Sometimes those appear. But the real value is not in individual interactions — it is in the accumulation.

The people who benefit most are not those who find a clever shortcut. They are the ones who apply the system consistently, refine how they work, and allow the effects to build. The partnership deepens. The thinking improves. The outputs become more aligned. The process becomes more natural. None of that happens instantly. All of it happens predictably if you stay with it.

This is not something you learn once. It is something you practise. The transformation happens in the sustained commitment, not the initial enthusiasm.

Try This Prompt
"I want to think about my AI partnership as a long-term investment, not a quick fix. Honestly assess: based on how I've been engaging, am I building something that will compound over time, or am I just extracting short-term value? Then help me design a realistic 12-month roadmap that builds genuine, compounding capability — not just productivity."
